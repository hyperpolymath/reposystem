# 🐾 *Voletaire, or The Cultivation of One’s Own Repository*

In the land of Gitagonia, where branches forked without purpose and commits bore no messages, there lived a young vole named **Voletaire**. Raised in the school of Pangitism, he was taught that “all repos are for the best in this best of all possible workflows.”

His mentor, **Professor Pushoptimus**, believed that merge conflicts were divine trials and that rebasing was a spiritual exercise. “Never question the structure,” he said. “The README shall reveal itself in time.”

But one day, Voletaire encountered a repo so tangled, so undocumented, so devoid of `.gitignore`, that even `ls` refused to list its contents. CI failed. Linting wept. The mascot had no name.

Thus began Voletaire’s journey.

He wandered through the **Monorepo of Babel**, where every language spoke a different dialect. He visited the **Temple of Terraform**, where infra was sacred but undocumented. He was nearly consumed by the **Recursive Loop of DevOps**, where pipelines deployed pipelines that deployed pipelines.

At last, he met a wise squirrel named **Gitocles**, who whispered:

> “The world is full of repos, Voletaire. But the only one you can truly cultivate is your own.”

Voletaire returned to Scaffoldia, built a validating registry, engraved dialectic lines into stone tablets, and declared:

> “Let us scaffold with clarity, and cultivate our own structure.”

---

## 🧠 Aphorisms from Voletaire

- “A repo without structure is like a novel without chapters - confusing, unreadable, and prone to merge conflicts.”
- “The best scaffold is not the most complex, but the most narratable.”
- “Let us define our folders, lest our folders define us.”
- “Nickel is the poetry of configuration—Dhall the grammar, Nix the punctuation.”
- “MiniKanren does not solve your repo—it reveals what your repo refuses to admit.”
- “CI/CD is not salvation. It is scaffolding.”
- “Let us not fork the world. Let us scaffold our own.”
